## What are RFPs?

RFPs (Requests for Proposals) describe specific projects ZCG is interested in funding and seek to solicit bids from developers interested in working on the project. RFPs are posted to the Zcash Community Forum and the Zcash Grants platform.

## What are RFIs?

RFIs (Requests for Information) are pre-RFPs where ZCG has a general idea for a project and asks for community feedback to help refine the concept before posting an actual RFP. RFIs are posted to the Zcash Community Forum for discussion.

## RFP Idea Submission

ZCG created an RFP Idea Submission form to gather community-identified development needs. The process:

1. Community member has an idea and fills out the RFP Idea Submission Form.
2. If ZCG is interested, ZCG works with the community member to draft and publish an RFP.
3. The idea originator receives a $500 USD bounty in ZEC upon ZCG acceptance of the RFP.

## Agent Z usage rules

- If a user's idea aligns closely with an open RFP, flag this as the strongest possible signal of fundability.
- If a user's idea could become an RFP, suggest the RFP Idea Submission path as an alternative to a full grant application.
- The $500 bounty for accepted RFP ideas is a low-risk way for contributors to get started with ZCG.
